## Computational Trust

The engineering instantiation of trust models in distributed and multi-agent systems: how an agent should update its degree of belief in another agent's reliability based on direct experience, witness reputation, sociological reputation, and prejudice. The dominant analytical decomposition is from [[Review on Computational Trust and Reputation Models]]; the operational principle behind most models is reciprocal updating in the spirit of [[Tit-for-Tat]]. Distinct from cryptographic trust (PKI, DIDs) — computational trust is about *behavioural* reliability, not cryptographic identity.
