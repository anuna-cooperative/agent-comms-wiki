# Accommodation

The pragmatic process (Lewis 1979, used by Stalnaker) whereby the conversational [[Common Ground]] is silently adjusted to make a speaker's move acceptable. When an utterance presupposes something not yet shared, hearers typically *accommodate* by adding the presupposition to the common ground rather than objecting — a key mechanism by which [[Update Semantics|context updates]] and [[Pragmatic Presupposition|presupposition]] actually work in dialogue.
