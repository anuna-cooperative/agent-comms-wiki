# Analytic-Synthetic Distinction

The purported division between statements true by virtue of meaning alone ("all bachelors are unmarried" — analytic) and those true by virtue of fact ("snow is white" — synthetic). Quine's *Two Dogmas of Empiricism* attacks it as the first "dogma": every attempt to define analyticity (via synonymy, definition, semantic rules) covertly presupposes the very notion, so the distinction is unfounded. Rejecting it leads to [[Semantic Holism]].
