# Logical Omniscience

The standard idealisation in [[Possible Worlds Semantics|possible-worlds]] models of knowledge: because an agent knows everything true in all worlds it considers possible, it automatically knows *all logical consequences* of its knowledge and every valid formula. This is unrealistic for resource-bounded reasoners (real agents do not instantly know every theorem entailed by their beliefs), and motivates weaker accounts — awareness logics, impossible-worlds, algorithmic/explicit knowledge — that relax closure under logical consequence.
