# Knowledge-Based Programs

A high-level specification style (Fagin, Halpern, Moses, Vardi) in which an agent's actions are conditioned on *explicit knowledge tests* — `if K_i(φ) then a` — rather than on concrete local-state values. The program says *what an agent must know* to act, abstracting away from how that knowledge is represented or attained; it is then implemented by a concrete protocol relative to a class of [[Interpreted Systems]]. Knowledge-based programs make the epistemic preconditions of coordination explicit and are a bridge from [[Epistemic Logic]] to executable distributed protocols.
