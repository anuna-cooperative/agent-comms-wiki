# Emergent Semantics

The view that semantic interoperability across large, decentralised collections of autonomous information sources is a *self-organising, bottom-up* property rather than a top-down precondition. Instead of legislating a single global schema or shared ontology, meaning is grown: sources form **local semantic agreements** (pairwise mappings, query translations, negotiated correspondences), and a network-wide semantics emerges as the aggregate, statistically consistent fixpoint of those local interactions. Draws on semiotics (meaning as a relation among symbols, referents, and a community of agents, fixed through use) and complex-systems self-organisation (global order as an emergent macro-property of micro-level interaction). The semantics is dynamic and self-referential — agreements are continually formed, tested, reinforced, or revised — so it is a drifting equilibrium, not a fixed artifact.
