# Transportability

The formal theory of when causal knowledge learned in one population or setting can be carried over to a different target population. Pearl and Bareinboim use *selection diagrams* (causal graphs annotated with nodes marking where the populations differ) to derive, via do-calculus, whether and how an effect estimated in the source licenses a valid estimate in the target. The external-validity counterpart to [[Identifiability]].
