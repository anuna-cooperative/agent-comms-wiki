# Autonomic Computing

A software-engineering tradition, launched by IBM's 2001 manifesto and crystallised in [[The Vision of Autonomic Computing]] (Kephart & Chess 2003), that responds to spiralling system complexity by building systems that **manage themselves** given high-level objectives. The biological metaphor is the autonomic nervous system: low-level regulation happens without conscious control so the administrator specifies *what* is wanted, not *how* to achieve it.

Autonomic systems are organised as [[MAPE-K]] loops — autonomic managers regulating managed elements — and are judged by the **self-* properties**: self-configuration, self-healing, self-optimisation, and self-protection (collectively "self-CHOP"). The tradition is the engineering counterpart to the biology-inspired [[Self-Adaptive Systems]] line already in this vault: where the latter asks *why* systems adapt, autonomic computing asks *how you build and assure* the adaptation.
