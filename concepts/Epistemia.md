# Epistemia

A structural condition in which **linguistic plausibility substitutes for epistemic evaluation** — coined in [[Epistemological Fault Lines Between Human and Artificial Intelligence]] (Quattrociocchi, Capraro & Perc 2025).

Under Epistemia, a generative system produces answers that are syntactically well-formed, semantically fluent, and rhetorically convincing without instantiating the processes by which beliefs are normally formed, tested, or revised. The user experiences the possession of an answer without traversing the cognitive labour of judgment.

Not reducible to [[Hallucination]], automation bias, or over-trust: Epistemia is an architectural phenomenon, not a user error. It persists even when systems are factually accurate — the harm is the structural bypassing of evaluation itself.
