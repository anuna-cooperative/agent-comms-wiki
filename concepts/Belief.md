## Belief

In agent theory, the propositional content an agent treats as (probably) true. The B in [[BDI]] / [[Belief-Desire-Intention]]; the operand of the modal operator `believes` in [[BAN Logic]] and the BDI-logic family ([[Cohen and Levesque|Cohen & Levesque]], [[Modeling Rational Agents within a BDI-Architecture|Rao & Georgeff]]). Distinguished from *knowledge* (which classically requires truth) and from *desire / goal*. The unit of [[Belief Revision]] under the AGM postulates. In [[Mentalistic Semantics]] the meaning of an ACL message is given in terms of the belief updates it induces in sender and receiver — the locus of [[ACL Rethinking Principles|Singh's]] critique that beliefs are unverifiable from message traces.
