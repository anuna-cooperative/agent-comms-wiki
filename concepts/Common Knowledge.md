# Common Knowledge

A group reaches *common knowledge* of φ when everyone knows φ, everyone knows that everyone knows φ, and so on to infinity (`C_G φ`). It is the epistemic precondition for coordinated simultaneous action, and the classic result of Halpern & Moses is that common knowledge **cannot be attained** in a system where communication is not guaranteed (the Coordinated Attack problem) — each message leaves a residual layer of uncertainty. Common knowledge is the strongest standard group-knowledge operator, above "everyone knows" and [[Distributed Knowledge]].
