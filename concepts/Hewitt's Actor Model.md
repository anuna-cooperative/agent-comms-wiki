## Hewitt's Actor Model

The original asynchronous-message-passing concurrency model proposed by Hewitt, Bishop & Steiger (1973). An actor is a stateful entity with a mailbox that, in response to a message, may: send a finite number of messages, create a finite number of new actors, and designate the behaviour for the next message. Universally cited as the predecessor of [[Communicating Sequential Processes|CSP]], [[CCS]], the [[Pi-Calculus]], and [[Programming Erlang Second Edition|Erlang]]; the asynchronous π-calculus and the [[Join Calculus]] supply formal accounts of actor-style concurrency that compose with bisimulation theory.
