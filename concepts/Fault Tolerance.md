# Fault Tolerance

The capacity of a system to continue correct operation despite failures of components. Von Neumann's [[Probabilistic Logics and the Synthesis of Reliable Organisms from Unreliable Components|1952 Caltech lectures]] gave the founding formal treatment, proving that arbitrarily reliable computation is achievable from components with per-operation error probability ε, provided ε < 1/6 and sufficient redundancy (triplication-and-majority-vote, or, far more efficiently, bundle multiplexing with restoring organs).
