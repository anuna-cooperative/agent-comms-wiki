## Nonmonotonic Reasoning

Reasoning in which adding new premises can *invalidate* previously-derivable conclusions — unlike classical logic, where the consequence relation is monotonic. The standard formalisations are McCarthy's [[Circumscription - A Form of Nonmonotonic Reasoning|circumscription]] (model-theoretic minimisation), Reiter's [[Default Logic]] (defaults as fixed-point operators), Moore's autoepistemic logic, and — the unifying framework — Dung's [[Argumentation Framework|argumentation frameworks]] under which nonmonotonic logics correspond to extension-acceptance computations.
