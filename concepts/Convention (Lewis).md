## Convention (Lewis)

David Lewis's (1969) game-theoretic analysis of conventions: a regularity `R` in the behaviour of a population is a *convention* iff almost everyone conforms to `R`, almost everyone expects almost everyone else to conform, almost everyone prefers conforming on the assumption that others conform, and some alternative regularity could equally have served. Sustained by [[Mutual Expectation]] / [[Common Knowledge (Convention)|common knowledge]]. Foundational analysis of how shared meaning, currency, and social roles arise without explicit agreement.
