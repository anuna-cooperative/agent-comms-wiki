## Replay Attack

An attack in which an adversary captures a legitimate protocol message and re-sends it later to obtain an effect that the original sender did not intend. The standard countermeasure is *freshness* — nonces, timestamps, sequence numbers — included in each protected message so that replays can be detected. Identified as a primary concern in [[Using Encryption for Authentication|Needham & Schroeder 1978]] and central to the design of every authentication protocol since.
