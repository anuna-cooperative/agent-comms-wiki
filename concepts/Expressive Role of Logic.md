# Expressive Role of Logic

Brandom's account of what logical vocabulary is *for*: not to extend our inferential powers but to make explicit, as assertible claims, the material inferential commitments that are otherwise only implicit in practice. The conditional lets one *say* "if p then q" what one previously only *did* in treating p as a reason for q. Logic is thus expressive rather than foundational — the organ by which a [[Discursive Practice]] becomes self-conscious.
