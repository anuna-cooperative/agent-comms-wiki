## Nonce

A *number used once* — a random or unique value generated by a protocol participant for a single protocol run, used to ensure freshness and prevent [[Replay Attack|replay]]. Including a fresh nonce in a message and requiring its echo back authenticates that the response was produced *after* the nonce was generated, defeating attempts to reuse old captured messages. Foundational construct in [[Using Encryption for Authentication|Needham-Schroeder]] and (under various names — challenge, salt, IV) in essentially every modern authentication protocol.
