# Social Norms

Shared, informally enforced expectations about how members of a group ought to behave — sustained not by a central authority but by mutual observation, reputation, and the threat of decentralized sanction (e.g. exclusion of defectors). In multi-agent settings, norms such as "cooperate with those who help others" can *emerge* from repeated interaction and reputational signalling rather than being imposed, which is the mechanism by which [[Indirect Reciprocity]] and gossip-based reputation stabilise cooperation among [[LLM Agents]].
