# Axiom of Choice

The axiom (AC) that for any collection of non-empty sets there exists a choice function selecting one element from each. Equivalent to the [[Well-Ordering Theorem]] and to Zorn's lemma. Made an explicit postulate by [[Untersuchungen über die Grundlagen der Mengenlehre I|Zermelo]], partly to ground his 1904 well-ordering proof against critics who objected to non-constructive choice. Independent of the other [[ZFC|ZF]] axioms: consistent (Gödel, via the [[Constructible Universe]]) and unprovable (Cohen, via [[Forcing]]).
