## Coordination Language

A language designed specifically to express the *coordination* (synchronisation, communication, composition) of concurrent activities, distinct from any *computation* language that would express the activities' internal logic. The framing is Gelernter's: a parallel/distributed program = computation + coordination. Examples: [[Linda (coordination)|Linda]] (1985), [[KLAIM]] (1998), [[Reo]] (2004), Manifold, GAMMA, Orc. Coordination languages are typically *embedded* — combined with a host computation language — and emphasise *separation of concerns* between what is computed and how concurrent computations interact.
