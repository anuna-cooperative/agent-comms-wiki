# Non-verbal Communication

Communicative behaviours that do not use a discrete symbolic channel — pointing, gaze, positioning. In Mordatch & Abbeel's emergent-language experiments agents spontaneously adopt non-verbal strategies when the symbolic channel is removed, illustrating communication as optimisation over all available action dimensions.
