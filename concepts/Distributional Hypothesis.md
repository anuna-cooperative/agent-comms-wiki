# Distributional Hypothesis

The claim — due to [[Distributional Structure|Harris (1954)]] and crystallised in Firth's slogan "you shall know a word by the company it keeps" — that **words occurring in similar contexts tend to have similar meanings**. Linguistic distribution (the set of contexts a unit appears in) is taken as an observable proxy for meaning, so semantic similarity can be estimated from co-occurrence statistics rather than from hand-built definitions. It is the theoretical premise underlying [[Distributional Semantics]] and modern [[Word Embeddings]].
