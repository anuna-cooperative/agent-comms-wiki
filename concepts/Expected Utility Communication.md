# Expected Utility Communication

A decision-theoretic framework for deciding *whether* and *what* to communicate: the utility of a message is the expected change in the hearer's mental state (and thus their action) weighed against production cost. Central to the automated evolution of ACLs where new lexical items are adopted when their expected utility exceeds implementation cost.
