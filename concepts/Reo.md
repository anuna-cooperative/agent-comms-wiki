## Reo

Arbab's (2004) channel-based [[Coordination Language]]: the primitive abstraction is a programmable [[Connector (Reo)|channel]] with typed source/sink endpoints (synchronous, lossy, FIFO, filter, drain, replicate, ...). Channels compose by node-sharing into composite connectors. Coordination logic lives *exogenously* in the connector network, separated from the components being coordinated. Compositional [[Constraint Automata|constraint-automata]] semantics (Baier et al. 2006). The algebraic counterpoint to [[Linda (coordination)|Linda]]'s tuple-space approach.
