# Repeated Game

A game in which the same stage game is played over many rounds by the same players, who can condition their actions on history. Repetition changes the strategic calculus: outcomes unreachable as equilibria of the one-shot game (notably mutual cooperation in a [[Social Dilemma]]) can be sustained because future reward and punishment discipline present behaviour — the content of the *folk theorems*. The [[Iterated Prisoners Dilemma]] and Axelrod's [[The Evolution of Cooperation|tournaments]] are the canonical studies, and repeated-game structure is what makes [[Reciprocity]] and [[Indirect Reciprocity]] effective among self-interested agents.
