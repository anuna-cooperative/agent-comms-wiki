# Empowerment

In normative-systems theory, the institutional capacity — conferred by [[Constitutive Rules|constitutive rules]] via the [[Counts-As Relation]] — for an agent's act to create institutional facts. A registrar is *empowered* so that their saying "I now pronounce you married" counts as marrying; without the empowering rule the same words have no institutional effect. Distinct from permission: one may be empowered to do what one is forbidden to do, and vice versa ([[Hohfeldian Analysis]]).
