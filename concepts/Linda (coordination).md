## Linda (coordination)

Gelernter's (1985) coordination language built around a shared content-addressable [[Tuple Spaces|tuple space]] accessed through four operations: `out` (write), `in` (consume on associative match), `rd` (read on match), `eval` (turn a tuple into a live computation). Decouples senders and receivers in space, time, destination, and form. The canonical example of *separation of computation and coordination*. Direct ancestor of JavaSpaces, TSpaces, [[KLAIM]], and stigmergic-coordination frameworks.
