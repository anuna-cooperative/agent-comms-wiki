## Focal Point

Schelling's (1960) concept: in a coordination game with multiple equilibria, the equilibrium parties converge on by virtue of *salience* — some feature that "stands out" in their shared informational and cultural background, distinguishing it from the others. Salience is not derivable from the payoff structure alone — it depends on context, prominence, simplicity, history. The cognitive mechanism by which Lewis-style [[Convention (Lewis)|conventions]] emerge without explicit agreement.
