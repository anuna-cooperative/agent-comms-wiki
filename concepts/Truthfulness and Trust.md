# Truthfulness and Trust

Lewis's pair of conventions that sustain a language L in a population: speakers are *truthful* in L (they assert only sentences they hold true in L) and hearers *trust* that they are (they form beliefs accordingly). The mutual expectation is self-perpetuating in the manner of a [[Convention (Lewis)|Lewis convention]], and it is what makes an abstract language the actual language *of* a community. Ancestor of trust-based accounts of [[Public Semantics]].
