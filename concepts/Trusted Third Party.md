# Trusted Third Party

A mutually-trusted intermediary that parties rely on to mediate an interaction (hold secrets, certify, compute over private inputs). Much of cryptography is the project of *removing* the TTP: [[Multi-Party Computation]], [[Zero-Knowledge Proofs|zero-knowledge proofs]], and trusted-execution environments each aim to deliver the guarantee a TTP would provide without requiring anyone to actually be trusted.
