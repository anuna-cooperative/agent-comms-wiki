## Common Knowledge (Convention)

The infinite hierarchy of mutual expectation introduced by Lewis (1969) and rediscovered by Aumann (1976) in game theory and Halpern–Moses (1990) in distributed systems: `A` knows `p`, `A` knows that `B` knows `p`, `A` knows that `B` knows that `A` knows `p`, … all the way up. A [[Convention (Lewis)|convention]] is sustained by common knowledge of the regularity. The construction has the same mathematical content in three independent fields — Lewis-style social philosophy, game theory, and distributed-systems epistemology — and is *unattainable* in fully asynchronous distributed systems (Halpern & Moses), with deep consequences for what coordination protocols can achieve.
