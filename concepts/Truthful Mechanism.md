## Truthful Mechanism

A mechanism in which truth-telling — reporting one's true type / valuation / preferences to the mechanism — is a (weakly) dominant strategy for every participant. Synonymous with [[Strategy-Proof]]. The canonical examples are the [[Vickrey Auction]] and its multi-item generalisation, the [[VCG Mechanism|Vickrey-Clarke-Groves mechanism]]. The Gibbard-Satterthwaite theorem shows that truthful mechanisms are severely restricted in general (only dictatorial mechanisms are truthful for unrestricted preferences with three or more outcomes), so truthful mechanism design typically restricts to specific structured domains (auctions, matching, public-goods provision).
