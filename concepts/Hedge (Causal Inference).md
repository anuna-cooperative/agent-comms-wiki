# Hedge (Causal Inference)

The graphical structure that witnesses *non-identifiability* of a causal effect. A hedge is formed by a pair of nested C-forests sharing a root set; Shpitser and Pearl prove that an interventional distribution is identifiable from observational data **iff** the causal graph contains no hedge for it. The [[ID Algorithm]] fails exactly when it encounters one, making the hedge the complete obstruction to identification.
