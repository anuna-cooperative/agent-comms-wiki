# Arrow Information Paradox

The classical paradox of information markets, identified by Kenneth Arrow (1962) and Richard Nelson (1959): a buyer cannot assess an idea's value without seeing it, yet revealing it removes the seller's leverage to be paid. Resolved in the agentic setting by [[NDAI Agreements|TEE-backed agreements]] and [[Trusted Capable Model Environment|capable-model intermediaries]] that act as trusted third parties.
