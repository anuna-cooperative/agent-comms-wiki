# Inferentialism

The semantic view, developed in its modern form by Robert Brandom (building on Sellars, Wittgenstein, and Quine), that the meaning of a sentence is its role in the *inferential* practices of a community of speakers — what asserting it entitles you to assert, what claims it is incompatible with, what would entitle you to assert it. Reference, truth, and representational content are downstream of inferential articulation rather than upstream of it. The slogan: *meaning is use*, formalised. The position is the philosophical underwriting of the [[Public Semantics]] / [[Commitment-based Semantics]] line in agent communication — a worked-out [[Semantics-Without-Minds]] that lets a wire-format trace carry full semantic content without invoking sender or receiver mental states.
