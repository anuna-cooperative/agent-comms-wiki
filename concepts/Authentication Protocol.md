## Authentication Protocol

A cryptographic protocol whose goal is to allow one principal to verify the identity of another (and typically to establish a shared session key for subsequent secure communication). Canonical examples: [[Needham-Schroeder Protocol]] (1978), Kerberos (1980s), TLS handshake, Signal X3DH/PQXDH, FIDO2/WebAuthn. Verification is the subject of [[BAN Logic]], the [[Spi Calculus]], and modern symbolic provers ([[ProVerif]], Tamarin) under the [[Dolev-Yao Model|Dolev-Yao threat model]].
