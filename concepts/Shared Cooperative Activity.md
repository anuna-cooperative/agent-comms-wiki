## Shared Cooperative Activity

Bratman's (1992) philosophical analysis of joint cooperative action: SCA requires (1) [[Mutual Responsiveness|mutual responsiveness]] to the other's intentions and actions, (2) commitment to the joint activity itself, (3) commitment to [[Meshing Subplans|mutual support]] should the other be unable to perform their part. Each agent's intentions are recursively nested in the other's. Distinguishes genuine cooperation from coincident individually-rational action; foundation of joint-intention models in MAS.
