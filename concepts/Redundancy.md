# Redundancy

The inclusion of more information or components than the minimum needed to perform a function, so that failures can be detected and masked. [[Probabilistic Logics and the Synthesis of Reliable Organisms from Unreliable Components|Von Neumann's 1952 lectures]] show how graceful redundancy yields arbitrarily reliable automata — by triplication-and-majority-vote (overhead 3^μ) or by bundle multiplexing with a restoring organ (overhead logarithmic in target reliability).
