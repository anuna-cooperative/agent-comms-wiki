# Well-Ordering Theorem

Every set can be well-ordered (given a total order in which every non-empty subset has a least element). Proved by Zermelo (1904) using the [[Axiom of Choice]], to which it is in fact equivalent. The controversy over its non-constructive use of choice is what led Zermelo to isolate AC as an explicit axiom in his [[Untersuchungen über die Grundlagen der Mengenlehre I|1908 axiomatization]]. Well-orderings are what the [[Ordinals]] are the order types of.
