# Free-Form Protocols

Open-ended, natural-language agent interactions that lack the rigid message schemas of classical [[Agent Communication Languages|ACLs]]. Their flexibility is also an attack surface: without fixed performatives or typed fields there is little to validate against, so injection, covert signalling ([[Secret Collusion]]), and protocol manipulation are harder to detect — one of the open problems the multi-agent-security agenda flags for LLM-mediated communication.
